Questions & Objections

Honest Questions Deserve Honest Answers

Many visitors will land here first — and that's alright. Below are the questions believers, skeptics, and the simply curious actually ask. Tap a question to expand it.

Isn't this just symbolic?

Answer coming soon. Starting point: look at Jesus' own words at the Last Supper and throughout John 6, and how the earliest Christians understood them — see About the Eucharist → Scripture.

Isn't this cannibalism?

Answer coming soon. Starting point: clarify what "eating flesh and blood" means here — the appearances remain bread and wine, and this isn't a literal, biological act in the sense the objection imagines.

Didn't Jesus say "Do this in remembrance of me"?

Answer coming soon. Starting point: explore what "remembrance" (anamnesis) meant in a Jewish liturgical context — not mere mental recall, but a ritual that makes a past saving event present again.

Why do Catholics worship the Eucharist?

Answer coming soon. Starting point: connect directly to belief in the Real Presence — if it is truly Christ, adoration follows. See About the Eucharist → Real Presence.

Why can't everyone receive Communion?

Answer coming soon. Starting point: see How to Receive → Who Can Receive for the unity-of-faith explanation — frame it charitably, not as exclusion for its own sake.

What if I doubt?

Answer coming soon. Starting point: doubt isn't the same as disbelief, and even saints have experienced it. Offer practical next steps — prayer, adoration, asking questions honestly.

Why did Jesus speak symbolically elsewhere?

Answer coming soon. Starting point: compare how Jesus signals figurative language elsewhere (parables, "I am the door") with the literalness and intensification of his words in John 6.

What happens if a Host falls?

Answer coming soon. Starting point: explain the careful procedures used (purification, consuming or dissolving a fragment) and why they follow directly from belief in the Real Presence.

Why do some Catholics receive on the tongue?

Answer coming soon. Starting point: connect to reverence and historical practice — see How to Receive → Receiving Reverently.

Do all Christians used to believe this?

Answer coming soon. Starting point: the patristic consensus on the Real Presence, and how and when some traditions diverged — see About the Eucharist → The Early Church.

What about Protestant interpretations?

Answer coming soon. Starting point: survey the major positions (e.g. consubstantiation/sacramental union, real spiritual presence, memorial views) accurately and charitably, without caricature.

Skeptics' Corner

Look Honestly, and Decide Where It Leads

The earliest disciples themselves struggled with these words. When Jesus first taught that his followers must eat his flesh and drink his blood, many of them walked away — not because they misunderstood him, but because they understood him perfectly. This page exists for anyone willing to look at that same teaching honestly today.

Two places to start: read the verse-by-verse walkthrough of John 6, and look at the documented Eucharistic miracles — then ask yourself what explanation best fits the evidence.
