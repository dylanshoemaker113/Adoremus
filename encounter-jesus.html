Eucharistic Adoration

Image: A monstrance exposed for Eucharistic Adoration

What It Is

Eucharistic Adoration is time spent in prayer before the Blessed Sacrament — either reserved quietly in the tabernacle, or exposed for a period of adoration in a monstrance (sometimes around the clock, in "perpetual adoration"). Stripped of all the vocabulary, it is simply time spent with Jesus.

Why Catholics Do It

If the Eucharist truly is Jesus — Body, Blood, Soul, and Divinity — then he can be visited, spoken to, and adored just as the apostles could approach him during his earthly life. Adoration takes that belief and acts on it.

What Happens During Adoration

There's no fixed script. Some people come for a few quiet minutes on the way to work; others commit to a regular weekly hour. Some pray aloud or with devotionals, others sit in silence. The Church doesn't require anything beyond presence — showing up is the prayer.

2

How to Make a Holy Hour

A "Holy Hour" is simply an extended time of prayer before the Blessed Sacrament. An hour can sound long — here are some ways to fill it.

Sit Silently

Simply being present is valid prayer. You don't need to perform, and you don't need to feel something for the time to "count."

Speak to Jesus

Talk to him as you would a trusted friend — about your day, your worries, your gratitude, your doubts. He already knows; he wants you to bring it to him anyway.

Read Scripture

Bring a Bible and read slowly, especially the Gospels. Let a phrase or image settle rather than rushing through chapters.

Pray the Rosary

The Rosary is a natural companion to adoration — meditating on the life of Christ while sitting in his actual presence.

Journal

Writing down what comes to mind — questions, gratitude, struggles, resolutions — can help a wandering mind settle and gives you something to look back on.

"I Don't Know What to Say"

It's completely normal to sit down in front of the Blessed Sacrament and feel… nothing. Or to not know what to say. A few things that help:

  • Give yourself permission to be bored or distracted — showing up is what matters, not how it feels.
  • Try repeating a short phrase, like "Jesus, I trust in you" or "Here I am, Lord," and let it become a kind of breathing.
  • Bring something to read — Scripture, a spiritual book, or the prayers below — if silence feels too hard at first.
  • Remember that an hour spent "doing nothing" in someone's presence is still real time together. The same is true here.

Spiritual Communion

What It Means

A spiritual communion is an act of faith, hope, and love — asking Jesus to come into your soul even when you cannot receive him sacramentally. It has been recommended by saints and spiritual writers for centuries: for those unable to attend Mass, those unable to receive Communion, or simply anyone wanting to deepen their desire for Christ throughout the day.

Traditional Prayer

My Jesus, I believe that You are present in the Most Holy Sacrament. I love You above all things, and I desire to receive You into my soul. Since I cannot now receive You sacramentally, come at least spiritually into my heart. I embrace You as if You were already there, and unite myself wholly to You. Never permit me to be separated from You. Amen.

— Traditional, after St. Alphonsus Liguori

Find Adoration Near You

Many parishes maintain a regular schedule of Eucharistic Adoration — weekly, daily, or "perpetual" (around the clock). A few practical ways to find adoration near you:

  1. Contact a local Catholic parish directly and ask about their adoration schedule.
  2. Check your diocese's website, which often lists perpetual adoration chapels.
  3. Look for a "Holy Hour" or "Adoration" listing in a parish bulletin.
